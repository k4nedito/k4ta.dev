# Pre-Rant about artificial and biological Neural Networks

*Below I have like light bulb over my head about subject I was subconsciously aware but never actually described it so, be prepared for that*

So i just randomly remembered how we used to study in post-soviet school, the process was simply memorizing one or two pages of the subject, word to word, then telling it to the teacher next day.

*Yeah, absolutely miserable experience, super turbo fucked up way to make youth dumber, but that's not what I'm going yap about.*

Instead, I also remember an interesting phenomenon that after a good night sleep, I could remember the memorized subject much better than previous night. 

There are like multitude of reasons for this, but on surface level it goes down to: **memory consolidation (transfer from short to long term storage), noise reduction and strengthening of important connections and fact that REM sleep helps with structure and meaning**

Now this is a big and complex part of the neuroscience, but a very specific part of that does overlap with what I do time to time, which is building neural networks and working on deep learning models in general, precisely the **noise reduction and strengthening of important connections**

Basically, during the sleep, your brain:

- strengthens neural connections that were used (what you studied for example)
- weakens irrelevant ones (yeah right?)

So, in laymen terms: **learning in the brain is literally the process of changing how neurons connect and signal to each other**, when we learn, what actually changes is:

- which neurons fire together
- how strongly they connect
- how easily one pattern triggers another

This means that, **learning is physical, not abstract. It is just rewiring process in your brain**

*This sorta blew my mind because regardless of the fact that I work on DL models and build NNs for my side projects, I always took it as extremely miniature representation of what is actually going on in your brain. Precisely I always thought that learning was abstract thing, but shit like - it's just rewiring. It behaves exactly like the artificial neural networks.* 

So the quick comparison chart looks like this:

- Neurons = nodes
- Synapses = weights
- Learning = adjusting those weights
- Practice/repetition = training iterations
- Sleep = batch update + cleanup

Key difference being fact that **AI changes weights using backpropagation, while brain changes synapses using chemistry and electrical activity but conceptually, you are training a biological neural network**.

# The Rant

Okay, now that we are over how LLMs and human brain does work in a same way, but LLMs are just large **LANGUAGE** models and nothing else, they do not compare to human brain in any other aspect and I'm tired of hearing how it's some artificial intelligence instead of just automated statistical model with shit ton of data. 

Humans did manage to replicate the core mechanism, but that's where the story ends pretty much. Yes LLMs and deep learning models do have learning substrate but that's like *having a brain stem, but expecting a mind*.

Let me actually propose my rant in a structured manner:

1. "AI" has no real world grounding 

Humans learn from:

- vision
- touch
- pain
- hunger
- social interaction
- physical causality 

"AI" learns from:

- text and images scraped from the internet 

It simply has **description of reality, not reality itself**.

Like it knows that *fire is hot*, but never experienced the *touching fire = damage* feeling, that literally breaks the generalization. 

2. "AI" has no persistent self-model

Humans have:

- a continuous body
- memory tied to identity
- goals across time
- consequences 

While AI:

- is reset per session
- has no true survival pressure
- no real long-term agency

**In order to have AGI, it requires a system that exists across time and learns from consequences. That's not entirely possible because it's risky, expensive and legally and ethically terrifying tbh**.

3. Scale!!!

Human brain is like 86 billion neurons working in a massive parallel system. Yeah it's slow but also extremely energy efficient and has evolved architecture (vision, language, emotion, motor).

Meanwhile, existing LLMs have like millions to maybe billions of parameters with flat architecture trained statistically on no evolutionary scaffolding.

We skipped 500 million years of architectural tuning that makes up a human.
